serenity March 8, 2017, 6:15am #2

We are starting with Holistic Management and the best guide I have found was curated by Dan Palmer from VEG - see here Our farm team (Kirsten and Jonathan and I) are going through this process at the moment. We are also working in a buddy system/peer support with Mel Turnbull who is using this tool on a non-farm related project. It is a very versatile planning tool. Maybe we could share our first draft / initial workings as we go on this forum?

I would also recommend a PDC as a baseline on the fundamentals if you havn’t already done one. Not sure when David Arnold is running another one but that would be my pick for extra value of being able to network within your region (and he’s awesome and the PDC is onsite on his small farm).

We also found Tammi and Stuart’s Grow Your Ethics workshop VERY valuable and also good way to network with other beginner farmers: http://jonaifarms.com.au/workshops/

And then books like Richard Perkins’ Making Small Farms Work and Grown and Gathered for inspiration and very practical suggestions re small farm context.

Fraser May 18, 2017, 9:51pm #4

Regrarians have an online course starting very soon. Incorporating aspects of permaculture design, keyline strategy and holistic management to build your farm plan and design. It’d definitely be worth looking up. It also depends if your farm plan is to be for an agricultural business, homesteading or somwhere bewteen the 2. When we started I tried to be the whole farm thing with all the animals and vegetables and nature corridors and tree crop plantings. Now I’m pretty much a straight up market gardener and I chip away on the other things from the centre out. It’s worth thinking about what products you want to and are able to sell then use that as your starting point.
